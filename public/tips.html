Cooking Tips

Simple tricks to boost flavor and save time in the kitchen.

Ingredient Substitutions

Don't have an ingredient? Many ingredients can be swapped for others in a pinch. For example:

  • 1 cup of sour cream can be substituted with 1 cup of plain yogurt
  • 1 clove of fresh garlic can be substituted with 1⁄8 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 cup of molasses can be substituted with 1 cup of honey
  • 1 cup of broth can be substituted with 1 cup of hot water and a bouillon cube
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch can be substituted with 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg can be substituted with 1⁄4 cup of applesauce or 1⁄4 cup of mashed banana

Food Equivalents

Understanding common food equivalents helps you measure better and avoid guesswork. For example:

  • 1 stick of butter is 8 tablespoons
  • 1 cup of egg whites is 8-10 whites
  • 16 graham crackers is about 1 cup of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 medium pineapple is about 3 cups of pineapple chunks
  • 1⁄3 - 1⁄2 cup of unpopped popcorn is about 8 cups of popped popcorn
  • 1 envelope of dry yeast is 2 1⁄4 teaspoons

Essential Kitchen Tools

Having the right tools in your kitchen can make cooking easier and more enjoyable. A few essentials to have are:

  • Chef’s Knife
  • Cutting Board
  • Measuring Cups & Spoons
  • Mixing Bowls
  • Wooden Spoon or Silicone Spatula
  • Nonstick Skillet or Frying Pan
  • Saucepan
  • Sheet Pan
  • Colander
  • Tongs
